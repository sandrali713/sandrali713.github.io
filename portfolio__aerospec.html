AeroSpec

Web Design

The Story

AeroSpec is a company created by the University of Washington's Electical Engineering Lab, the Sensors, Energy, and Automation Labratory, otherwise known as SEAL. We're promoting and selling a sensor system for both companies and individual users who are concerned about their health and the quality of the air they're breathing. Our group is small but it includes a team for firmware, hardware, PCB design, marketing, and website development and design.

Brief

As part of the website design team, I worked with two masters students closely on our design, while meeting with the website development team regularly. As a group, we decided on all our potential users and what they might need. We then sorted their needs into categories to make an information architecture (IA). From there, I focused mainly on the UI/UX aspect, creating wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes for the screens we would need.

Below is the sitemap we created based on the IA. As you can see, it contains a lot of different parts. Because our website will be heavy information visualization, and has at least two distinct users that will have unique needs, there are a lot of different pages we will design.

Information Architechture

We created this IA by first coming up with a list of all the possible users that might use our product. We then sorted these users into categories, and found that most people fell into one of two categories: they either had a business, and wanted to monitor a large number of sensors distributed through a building, or wanted it for personal use to monitor themselves or their home. Under the personal group, we outlined some more specific users who we considered "sensitive groups", which included people with Asthma (or other breathing conditions), young children, and the elderly.

Next, for each user we defined, we brainstormed all the functions they might need. A company user might want to compare 10 different sensors at once for the last 24 hours, while an end user might want to see how location affect their air quality as they go on their morning run. Both users might not know what a safe PM level is or might want to read more about what types of PM there are. After we defined all the user functionalities, we used card sorting to group them. The data from the card sorting was the basis for our IA, and after some discussion and tweaking, this is what we came up with.

Design System

While creating the low-fi prototypes, we realized we needed more visual guidelines to keep our designs consistent. We decided to create a design system with colors, fonts, and basic elements like buttons and hovers. This way, our web development team would also have an easier time creating the website.

We started out with a the basics, like fonts and colors, and then continued to build elements so that the color scheme and fonts stayed consistent. We wanted the AeroSpec logo to have a whimsical, airy feel, and also connote feelings of health and nature. We also wanted a modern and professional feel for the rest of the website. Challenges

This project has had countless challenges and probably will have a lot more. Personally, this was my first time going in-depth with Figma, so it's been a learning process for me. However, the program is very intuitive and my teammates are always available when I have questions.

Overall, there's a lot of data visualization that goes into the dashboards, which has been a struggle. Each sensor measures about 10 different components, and a factory user can place dozens of sensors on each floor. They need to know how each element of each sensor changes with time, how they compare to each other, and even how they compare over time. They also need to know when alerts come up for each sensor, what type of alert it is, and if it still needs to be addressed. Lastly, they need an overview of a summary or average of the sensors so they can get idea of what's going on at a glance. Organizing this information so that it's easy for the user to comprehend is one of the biggest challenges we're currently working on.

Reflection

This project is still a work in progress, and it's by far the biggest venture I've ever worked on. It's essentially a startup, where we have a product we're trying to sell and we need to understand our users to create an efficient and intuitive interface for them. We've done countless iterations for each page we design, and we plan to do many more before anything is finalized. We've still got a lot of pages to go, and this product may take many more months before it's finished.

I've learned a lot from this project, like how to design effectively for different teams to use our design (like web development and business), to what makes a design intuitive, to what things are important about a product to highlight on a landing page. I've gotten a chance to learn how to use Figma, how to develop and use a design system, and so much more. I've also gotten to practice a lot of soft skills, like teamwork and communication, critique, and time management. Working on this project has not only made a better designer, but a better teammate as well.
