Muse Media

Layout Design

Muse Media was created in the fall of 2017, my freshman year of college. Its goal was to give creatives on campus an outlet to express themselves, their ideas, and to share it with others. It's filled with people who love to make all kinds of art and media - from fashion to photography, graphic art to poetry, or anything else. Last year, in 2018, we created the University of Washington's first fashion magazine. It was a huge task, and took nearly the whole school year, but also a ton of fun.

The Magazine

Creating our first magazine was undoubtedly a big task. We found that there was a lot more to it than just content - we needed a budget, sponsors, promotions, and a lot more. Our president divided up these tasks and created officers responsible for these aspects of the magazine. These positions included the marketing/social media officer, the finance/fundraising officer, and the graphic design officer (which I will be leading next year). After months of hard work, we had our official launch party April, 2019, and have released our first issue both in print and online, which can be viewed in the link above.

Challenges

The process of creating a magazine from scratch wasn't easy. We had a small team of about 10-30 people, depending on the day, and accountability was a challenge as a student-run organization. However, we did our best to enforce deadlines with constant reminders and nudges.

Creating a theme for the magazine was also a challenge. We wanted it to be general enough for plenty of articles within the theme, and many interpretations, but specific enough for all the pieces to be related. We also wanted it to be relevant to our generation and important enough to be for a first issue. After lots of brainstorming, we settled on the theme of identity, since it was relatable for so many of both with the club and for those reading it.
